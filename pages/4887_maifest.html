What Is Maifest?

It's not May in Bavaria until the Maibaum stands.

For centuries, Bavarian villages have raised the Maibaum — the Maypole — on the first of May to mark the arrival of spring. Painted in blue and white, wrapped in ribbons, and crowned with a wreath of fresh green, each pole is a point of pride for the town that raises it.

Tradition says neighboring villages will try to steal yours. If they do, you owe them beer and food to get it back. So once it's up, it's guarded around the clock — with plenty of Maibock to keep the watchmen going.

Der Maibaum

The Centerpiece of Our Maifest

Our authentic Bavarian-style Maibaum stands tall over the Biergarten year-round — painted in classic blue and white, adorned with the Zunftzeichen (guild signs) of traditional trades, and crowned with a Maikranz wreath trailing ribbons in the breeze. On Maifest day, our Schuhplattler dancers circle the pole with the traditional Maibaum Dance — braiding ribbons into a woven pattern, then unwinding them back out. It's a living piece of Bavaria, right here in Pinellas Park. And with our new authentic Biergarten furniture, you will be transported into a traditional German Biergarten!

Maifest Tradition

Maibock

The beer that gives the season its name

Bright golden, crisp, and lightly hoppy — Maibock is a spring bock brewed stronger than a Helles but cleaner than a dunkel. Bavarian monks first brewed it to mark the end of Lent and the start of warmer days. Today it's synonymous with Maifest itself. Poured fresh into your stein at the Biergarten bar.

Also available: a selection of wine, schnapps, and soft drinks.
